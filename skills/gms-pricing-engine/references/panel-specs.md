## Panel Category Mapping

### Exposed Fastener (EF) Panels — 35% Target Margin

**Installation:** Fasteners visible and penetrate through the rib top. Simpler installation, lower service complexity.

**Panels in category:**
- Classic Rib
- PBR (Pro Rib)
- Pro Panel (R-Panel)
- Ag Panel (A9 and A6 variants)

**Key characteristics:**
- Fasteners exposed to weather
- Easier installation (hand tools only)
- Lower material cost
- Higher volume, commodity-like pricing
- Best for agricultural, industrial, utility buildings

---

### Hidden Fastener (HF) Panels — 40% Target Margin

**Installation:** Fasteners concealed. Requires clips (standing seam) or mechanical fastening (flat systems). More complex installation = higher value.

**Panels in category:**
- SSQ550 (Standing Seam — Snap-Lock)
- SSQ675 (Standing Seam — Snap-Lock)
- FF100 (Flush Flat — Mechanical)
- BBQ750 (Board & Batten — Hidden)

**Key characteristics:**
- Concealed fasteners provide superior aesthetics
- Floating clips accommodate thermal movement (1/8" per 40 feet)
- More labor-intensive installation
- Premium positioning
- Best for residential, commercial, high-end applications

## Special Installation Notes

### Standing Seam Panels (SSQ550, SSQ675)
1. **Requires clips:** Fixed clips at ridge/eave + floating clips every 24" O.C.
2. **Underlayment critical:** Ice/water shield at eaves, synthetic underlayment elsewhere
3. **Never use felt:** Bituminous felt deteriorates and causes streaking
4. **Thermal movement:** Allow 1/8" per 40 feet (clips float to accommodate)
5. **Hand seaming only:** No mechanical seaming equipment required (snap-lock system)
6. **Solid substrate:** Plywood/OSB decking required (or 24" max purlin spacing)

### Board & Batten (BBQ750)
1. **Trim family critical:** MUST use complete B&B trim system (BBJT, BBBT, BBDF, BBHT, BBOC, BBIC, etc.)
2. **Battle over base:** Battens snap/clip over flat base panels
3. **All fasteners hidden:** No exposed screws
4. **Premium labor:** More time-intensive than snap-lock standing seam
5. **Aesthetics:** Batten lines create dramatic shadow lines

### Exposed Fastener Panels (Classic Rib, PBR, Pro Panel, Ag)
1. **Closure strips required:** At eaves and ridges to prevent water infiltration
2. **Anti-siphon detail:** Most panels have anti-siphon side lap groove
3. **Fastener spec:** #12 × 1-1/4" self-drilling with EPDM washer (critical detail)
4. **Don't over-tighten:** Snug + 1/4 turn ideal; over-driving compresses washer and causes leaks
5. **Cold weather:** Pre-drill in cold weather to prevent EPDM washer cracking
6. **Storage:** Stack on edge, not flat (prevents oil-canning)

### Flush Flat (FF100)
1. **Mechanical seaming required:** Not snap-lock; requires clips + fasteners
2. **Steeper slope:** 3:12 minimum (vs. 1:12 for standing seam)
3. **Thermal movement:** Limited accommodation compared to floating clip systems
4. **Aesthetic advantage:** Zero oil-canning visibility on wide flats (flat profile)

