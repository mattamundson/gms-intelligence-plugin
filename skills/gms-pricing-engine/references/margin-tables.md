# GMS Margin Targets & Health Thresholds

## Target Margins by Category

Greenfield Metal Sales uses a **two-tier margin system** based on product type and complexity.

### Exposed Fastener Panels (35% Target)
Metal-to-metal fastening with visible fasteners. Simpler installation = lower service risk = 35% margin.

| Category | Product Examples | Margin Target | Sell Price Formula | Notes |
|----------|------------------|----------------|-------------------|-------|
| **Exposed Fastener** | Classic Rib, PBR, Pro Panel, FF100 (29, 26, 24 ga) | **35%** | Cost ÷ 0.65 | Installed margin: 35% |

**Examples:**
- Material cost: $2.00/LF → Sell price: $2.00 ÷ 0.65 = **$3.08/LF** (35% margin)
- Material cost: $3.50/LF → Sell price: $3.50 ÷ 0.65 = **$5.38/LF** (35% margin)

---

### Hidden Fastener Panels (40% Target)
Concealed fastening system (standing seam, clip-dependent). More complex installation = higher value = 40% margin.

| Category | Product Examples | Margin Target | Sell Price Formula | Notes |
|----------|------------------|----------------|-------------------|-------|
| **Hidden Fastener** | SSQ550, SSQ675, Trapezoid, B&B (26, 24 ga) | **40%** | Cost ÷ 0.60 | Installed margin: 40% |

**Examples:**
- Material cost: $2.50/LF → Sell price: $2.50 ÷ 0.60 = **$4.17/LF** (40% margin)
- Material cost: $4.00/LF → Sell price: $4.00 ÷ 0.60 = **$6.67/LF** (40% margin)

---

### Trim & Flashing (50% Target)
Small linear footage items, high material markup, low labor intensity. Highest margin category.

| Category | Product Examples | Margin Target | Sell Price Formula | Notes |
|----------|------------------|----------------|-------------------|-------|
| **Trim & Flashing** | All FAS family (flashings, trim, special cuts) | **50%** | Cost ÷ 0.50 | Supply-only (no installation) |

**Examples:**
- Material cost: $1.00/LF → Sell price: $1.00 ÷ 0.50 = **$2.00/LF** (50% margin)
- Material cost: $1.50/LF → Sell price: $1.50 ÷ 0.50 = **$3.00/LF** (50% margin)

## Margin Health Classification

### Health Score System

```
🟢 GREEN (Excellent): >45%
   Action: Standard pricing approved
   Notes: Above target — possible optimization opportunity

🟢 GREEN (Good): 40-45%
   Action: Standard pricing approved
   Notes: At or above target

🟡 YELLOW (OK): 35-40%
   Action: Review and monitor
   Notes: Below target — check cost inputs and finish premiums

🟠 ORANGE (Low): 25-35%

   Notes: Significantly below target — likely pricing/cost error
```

## Margin Calculation Formulas

### Margin (as percentage)
```
Margin % = ((Sell Price - Cost) / Sell Price) × 100
```

**Example:**
- Sell Price: $3.08
- Cost: $2.00
- Margin: (($3.08 - $2.00) / $3.08) × 100 = 35.06%

### Inverse: Solve for Sell Price
```
Sell Price = Cost / (1 - Margin%)
```

**Example (35% margin target):**
- Cost: $2.00
- Margin %: 0.35
- Sell Price: $2.00 / (1 - 0.35) = $2.00 / 0.65 = $3.08

### Inverse: Solve for Target Cost
```
Target Cost = Sell Price × (1 - Margin%)
```

**Example (35% margin on $3.08 sell price):**
- Sell Price: $3.08
- Margin %: 0.35
- Target Cost: $3.08 × (1 - 0.35) = $3.08 × 0.65 = $2.00

## Multi-Product Order Margin Calculation

For orders with mixed product types, calculate blended margin:

```
Blended Margin % = (Total Margin $ / Total Sell Price) × 100
```

**Example order:**
- 100 LF Classic Rib @ $3.08/LF = $308 sell, $200 cost = $108 margin (35%)
- 50 LF SSQ550 @ $4.17/LF = $208.50 sell, $125 cost = $83.50 margin (40%)
- 25 LF Trim @ $2.00/LF = $50 sell, $25 cost = $25 margin (50%)

```
Blended: ($108 + $83.50 + $25) / ($308 + $208.50 + $50) = $216.50 / $566.50 = 38.2%
```

**Interpretation:** Despite mixed products, order maintains healthy 38.2% blended margin (above 35% floor).

## Margin Drivers & Sensitivity

### What increases margin?
1. **Higher coil cost** → Triggers premium finish upcharge
2. **Bulk orders** → Allows efficiency discount offset by higher volume
3. **Standard finishes** → Lower material cost (Galvalume, SMP, Acrylic)
4. **Trim products** → 50% target vs 35-40% for panels

### What decreases margin?
1. **Premium finishes** → Kynar PVDF, Camo, Woodgrain add $0.50-$2.36/LF
2. **Short runs** → Labor cost per LF increases
3. **Gauge upgrades** → 29GA → 26GA → 24GA = cost jumps
4. **Special cuts** → Non-standard widths or lengths (less coil efficiency)

### Coil Cost Impact on Margins
```
Example: 26GA-20-G50 trim @ 50% margin

Cost scenario 1:
- Coil base: $1.37 → Sell: $2.74 → Margin: 50%

Cost scenario 2 (coil +$0.10):
- Coil base: $1.47 → Sell: $2.94 → Margin: 50%
- (Price adjustment required to maintain margin)

Cost scenario 3 (coil +$0.20, no price increase):
- Coil base: $1.57, Sell: $2.74 → Margin: 43%
- ⚠️ Margin degradation
```

**Lesson:** Monitor coil costs monthly. When coil costs rise >$0.10, review pricing to maintain target margins.
